Business Leadership: Management Fundamentals

Grade 12, University/College Preparation — Explore leadership skills, management functions, ethics, and how to effectively lead people and organizations in the business world.

Unit 1: Foundations of Management Unit 2: Leading Unit 3: Management Challenges Unit 4: Planning & Controlling Unit 5: Organizing

Course Overview

This business course focuses on the development of leadership skills used in managing a successful business. Students will analyse the role of a leader in business, with a focus on decision making, management of group dynamics, workplace stress and conflict, motivation of employees, and planning. Effective business communication skills, ethics, and social responsibility are also emphasized.

What is leadership? How do people effectively manage others? In this course, you will explore the development of leadership skills and how these skills are used to effectively manage people and companies. You will also understand how managers function within organizations and solve real world problems. Emphasis will be placed on the importance of ethics and social responsibility.

Foundations of Management
5 Lessons + Unit Highlights + Test
▼
Welcome to Unit 1. In this unit, we will be examining how businesses are organized. We will start by looking at trends that affect organizations and by assessing the role of management. Our skills focus for this unit will be on developing appropriate communication techniques specific to business management. You will use a variety of concepts and theories to develop business documents and presentations. Finally, in our ever-changing business world, we will evaluate the impact of ethics and social responsibility on company management.

Overall Expectations / Learning Objectives:

  • Assess the role of management within an organization
  • Demonstrate the use of appropriate communication techniques related to business management
  • Evaluate the impact of issues related to ethics and social responsibility on organizational management
1
1-1: What's Happening in Business?
▼
"A business that makes nothing but money is a poor kind of business." — Henry Ford
Businesses have changed significantly over the past 100 years, and for that matter, the way of doing business today is dramatically different than even 20 years ago. Technology has been one of the major drivers of the change taking place in businesses. As more sophisticated technology enables business to be conducted more effectively and at an ever-greater pace, it also has downsides — the number of scandals and ethical violations has also risen.
Key Idea

This lesson will help you understand and appreciate the various ethical issues and challenges that are prevalent in the business world today. The way businesses deal with these issues affects not only them and their employees, but society at large.

Learning Goals
  1. Understand major ethical issues facing businesses and their impact on society
    • I can research major ethical issues facing businesses in today's world
    • I can summarize the major ethical issue in the scandal in one or two sentences
  2. Understand ethics and how ethical dilemmas impact various stakeholders
    • I can explain the meaning of ethics and what is considered ethical behaviour
    • I understand the difference between terminal and instrumental values
    • I can state and explain the four views of ethical behaviour
    • I understand the difference between cultural relativism and universalism
  3. Understand ethical dilemmas in the workplace
  4. Use the ethical decision making model to make a decision in a variety of business scenarios
  5. Identify potential sources of ethical issues in the workplace
  6. Evaluate ethical dilemmas facing businesses using the ethical decision-making model
  7. Assess the role management plays in creating and dealing with ethical issues
    • I can explain the meaning of a whistleblower
    • I can understand what a Code of Ethics is and the areas covered in it
  8. Understand the process in responding to ethical issues (six steps or questions)
  9. Evaluate the impact of major ethical issues on management strategies and decision-making
Scandal Ethics Ethical Behaviour Ethical Dilemma Whistleblower Code of Ethics
Higher Order Thinking Skills (HOTS)
  • Critically thinking about ethical dilemmas and ethical decision making
  • Communicate your understanding of the underlying ethical issues

"Tend to the people, and they will tend to the business." — John Maxwell
Managers play an integral role in any organization. In this lesson, we will understand the fundamentals of management that are important for business success — the types of managers, the levels of management, and the functions of management.

Understand basic aspects of management. Once we grasp these concepts, we will explore organizations, their characteristics, and features. We will conclude by examining the roles and responsibilities of managers within organizations.

  2. Understand the roles and responsibilities at each level of management
    • I can explain the three levels of managers in an organization
  3. Identify the different types of managers that exist within organizations
  4. Apply the three characteristics of an organization to real examples
    • I can explain Purpose, Division of Labour, and Hierarchy of Authority
  5. Evaluate and explain the role managers play within organizations
    • I can explain the main managerial skills
    • I can evaluate the roles managers play in organizations
Management Administrator Functional Manager General Manager Line Manager Staff Manager Organization Division of Labour Hierarchy of Authority
Higher Order Thinking Skills (HOTS)

  • Apply management characteristics to real world organizations
  • Analyze the functions of management

"My job is not to be easy on people. My job is to take these great people we have and to push them and make them even better." — Steve Jobs

  2. Explain how corporate culture affects organizations

    • I can identify different types of corporate culture in different organizations
  3. Evaluate major management theories and practices
    • I understand the six major theories of management

Corporate Culture Scientific Management Administrative Management Behavioural Management
